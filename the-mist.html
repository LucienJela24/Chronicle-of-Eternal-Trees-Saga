The Roots of the Tree

The Mist is the deepest domain of the Eastern Plane, the Roots of the Tree of Order. It is the realm of the Third Persona — the cosmic principle of Life and Death — and the sacred boundary where souls journey between incarnations. Here dwell the Life Bringers and Death Reapers, the adjudicators who record births, guide the dying, and maintain the balance of mortality.

Unlike the mortal fear of death, the Mist is not a place of punishment but of consequence. It gives meaning to finitude. Deep within its silver fog lies Tartarus, the prison of eternal stillness where beings who cannot be killed — such as the reformed Dark Emperors and the Inquisitor — are held in silent meditation. The Mist remembers every soul that has passed through it, and the echoes of their lives whisper in the fog.

Life Bringers & Death Reapers

Auriel

Realm Life Bringer of Lux Imperio

A figure of alternating light and shadow, robed in gold and silver threads. One hand holds a blossom that blooms and withers endlessly — the cycle of life made visible. Auriel is present at every birth in the Northern realm, recording the soul's entry into the world. She counseled Pope Celestine IV at his death and guided him into the Mist.

"Life is not a gift to be hoarded, but a breath to be returned."

Umbra

Realm Death Reaper of the East (Chuugoku)

An old man in grey robes, face hidden, who counts the dead. During the Council-Tribunic Wars, Umbra walked the battlefields of the East, noting every fallen soldier with silent precision. He reports to Auriel and ensures that no soul is lost to the Void — every death is witnessed, every life accounted for.

"I do not judge. I count. The judgment belongs to the living."

Tartarus: The Deep Prison

In the deepest, stillest part of the Mist lies Tartarus — a dimension of eternal silence where beings who cannot be killed are sealed. It is neither life nor death, but a state of suspended consequence. Here reside the reformed Dark Emperors (Tomoyama, Kross, Marcus), who lost their corruption centuries ago and now serve their sentences in peaceful meditation. The Inquisitor Redford Heinsforth was also cast here after his trial.

Tartarus is not a place of torture; it is a place of stillness. The Keepers and Death Reapers maintain the seals. Lucius Arogzan visited Tartarus briefly in Year 8223 and spoke with the reformed Emperors, who confirmed that their corruption had burned out, leaving only the weight of memory.

The Seventh Clue: Transcendence

In Book 4 (The Wanderer's Path), after receiving the sixth clue (Absence) in the Celestial Realm, Lucius descended into the Mist. Here he met Auriel and the 10th Guardian, Ada, who had placed the eight clues five thousand years earlier. Ada revealed that the seventh clue was not an object but a direction — pointing to the Great Forest (Branches). The clue represented TRANSCENDENCE, the node of going beyond, service, and sacrifice.

The lesson of the Mist: "Transcendence is not rising above but going through." To reach the center, Lucius had to pass through the roots of existence — the place where life ends and begins again. Ada could not complete the path herself, but she ensured that the Wanderer would find the way.
