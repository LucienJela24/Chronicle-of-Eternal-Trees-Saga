Overview

The Zhongguo Continent represents a fascinating cultural fusion zone in the Western Plane, where Eastern traditions brought by migrants and traders have merged with indigenous Western practices to create unique political and social systems. The continent's name reflects its identity as the "Central Kingdom" — Zhongguo — a land of democratic cultivation, elemental harmony, and scholarly pursuit.

Four major eras have shaped the continent: the Xi Dynasty (Absolute Years 20,001–20,212), the first unified empire; the Five Kingdoms Era (Absolute Years 20,212–21,706), a period of divided sovereignty; the Ming Dynasty (Absolute Years 21,706–49,636), the great synthesis empire; and the Union Era, representing the apex of democratic cultivation governance.

The continent's strategic location makes it a crossroads of trade and cultural exchange. Its seven prefectures — Central (Zhongzhou), Eastern (Donghai), Northeastern (Liaodong), Southeastern (Wuyue), Southern (Nanping), Southwestern (Bashu), and Western (Xiguan) — each maintain distinct industries and cultivation traditions while united under democratic governance.

The Four Powers

Xi Dynasty

Eastern Imperial Tradition

The Xi Dynasty maintains the most purely Eastern-style governance on the continent, having been founded by migrants from the Eastern Plane who sought to preserve their traditions in a new land. The Dynasty emphasizes imperial authority, bureaucratic governance, and Confucian social values.

11-Framework Analysis

1. Historical

Founded six centuries ago by refugees from the Eastern Plane. Maintained unbroken succession through careful bloodline management.

2. Political

Absolute monarchy with the Emperor as Son of Heaven. Six ministries handle administration, revenue, rites, war, justice, and public works.

3. Mental

Education emphasizes classical texts and civil service examination preparation. Imperial exams select officials based on merit.

4. Spiritual

Ancestor worship central to spiritual life. Emperor performs rituals to maintain cosmic harmony. Buddhism and Taoism coexist.

5. Social

Strict hierarchy: scholars, farmers, artisans, merchants. Family foundation of society with filial piety as highest virtue.

6. Cultural

Rich traditions of poetry, painting, music emphasizing harmony. Tea ceremonies and seasonal festivals mark the calendar.

7. Economical

Agricultural foundation with rice cultivation. State monopolies on salt, iron, and essentials. Trade regulated but flourishes.

8. Physiological

Traditional medicine combining herbal remedies, acupuncture, and energy cultivation. Martial arts encourage physical fitness.

9. Territorial

Largest territory on continent. Capital features Forbidden Palace. Regional capitals administer provinces.

10. Technological

Advanced agriculture, canals, dams, irrigation. Paper, printing, compass originated here. Gunpowder reserved for military.

11. Military

Large standing army organized into regional commands. Imperial Guard protects capital. Military exams parallel civil exams.

Five Kingdoms

Mixed Heritage Confederation

A confederation of five smaller kingdoms — Qi, Chu, Zhao, Han, and Ming — each with its own blend of Eastern and Western cultural elements. The Five Kingdoms cooperate on defense and trade while maintaining distinct identities and internal autonomy.

11-Framework Analysis

1. Historical

Emerged from fragmentation of a larger state three centuries ago. Formed confederation for mutual benefit rather than continued warfare.

2. Political

Each kingdom governed by its own monarch. Council of Kings coordinates confederation policy. Consensus required for decisions.

3. Mental

Education varies by kingdom, blending Eastern and Western approaches. Cross-border study encouraged to foster understanding.

4. Spiritual

Religious diversity is the norm. Each kingdom has own spiritual traditions. Religious tolerance a confederation principle.

5. Social

Social structures vary between kingdoms. Intermarriage created blended aristocracy. Common people identify with kingdom first.

6. Cultural

Distinct traditions in each kingdom. Art, music, cuisine show both Eastern and Western influences. Cultural exchange celebrated.

7. Economical

Free trade between kingdoms guaranteed. Each specializes in different goods. Combined economic weight is significant.

8. Physiological

Medical practices vary. Cross-border knowledge sharing has improved healthcare throughout the confederation.

9. Territorial

Each kingdom maintains traditional territory with clearly defined borders. Neutral central city hosts confederation institutions.

10. Technological

Technology sharing accelerates innovation. Some excel in metallurgy, others in textiles or agriculture.

11. Military

Each kingdom maintains own forces trained to coordinate. Confederation Guard serves as rapid reaction force.

Ming Dynasty

The Synthesis Empire

The Ming Dynasty represents the great synthesis — ruling for nearly 28,000 years from Absolute Year 21,706 to 49,636. As the seat of the Chen Master's Pavilion and the Four Schools Academy, the Ming Dynasty achieved the most complete integration of Eastern and Western cultivation traditions in the continent's history.

11-Framework Analysis

1. Historical

Founded four centuries ago by a rebellious general. Fought several wars with the Xi Dynasty. Each claims legitimacy over all Zhongguo.

2. Political

Imperial system with Western-influenced legal reforms. Emperor shares power with Grand Council. Provincial governors have autonomy.

3. Mental

Combines Eastern classics with Western sciences and philosophy. Civil service exam includes practical subjects. Critical thinking encouraged.

4. Spiritual

Promotes reformed religion incorporating Western philosophy. Religious tolerance greater than Xi Dynasty. State maintains temples.

5. Social

Less rigid hierarchy than Xi Dynasty. Merchants enjoy higher status. Women have more opportunities including education and property.

6. Cultural

Distinct artistic tradition blending Eastern aesthetics with Western techniques. Porcelain, silk, and jade work highly valued.

7. Economical

Commercial economy with active domestic and international trade. Banking and credit well-developed. Seeks Western Plane trade.

8. Physiological

Combines traditional medicine with Western surgery. Public health measures include sanitation and quarantine. Martial arts and sports promoted.

9. Territorial

Controls southern portion with major trade routes. Capital is cosmopolitan with Western influence. Border fortifications face Xi Dynasty.

10. Technological

Adopts Western technologies while preserving Eastern innovations. Shipbuilding advanced. Research institutions study both traditions.

11. Military

Modernized forces combining Eastern and Western techniques. Navy strong, protecting trade routes. Firearms integrated with traditional forces.

The Union

Democratic Cultivation Republic

The most successful example of East-West political fusion on the continent, the Union combines Eastern-style meritocratic bureaucracy with Western democratic institutions. This hybrid system — Democratic Cultivation — has created a prosperous and stable state that serves as a model for others.

11-Framework Analysis

1. Historical

Founded two centuries ago by reformers combining Eastern and Western governance. Constitution considered a masterpiece of political philosophy.

2. Political

Federal republic with elected legislature and meritocratic civil service. Chancellor elected by Assembly. Examination Board selects officials.

3. Mental

Universal education emphasizing Eastern and Western knowledge. Critical thinking and civic responsibility are core values.

4. Spiritual

Complete religious freedom with no state religion. All faiths equal under law. Interfaith dialogue actively promoted.

5. Social

High social mobility based on merit. Equality before law guaranteed. Traditional family values coexist with progressive policies.

6. Cultural

Vibrant fusion culture. Art, music, literature blend Eastern and Western forms. Cultural exports popular across the planes.

7. Economical

Mixed economy with state enterprises and private business. Open trade policy. Innovation encouraged and rewarded.

8. Physiological

Universal healthcare combining Eastern and Western medicine. Advanced public health. Life expectancy among highest on continent.

9. Territorial

Western coastal territory with excellent ports. Planned capital city embodies Union principles. Infrastructure well-maintained.

10. Technological

Leader in innovation combining Eastern and Western approaches. Patents protect inventors. Magical and mundane tech integrated.

11. Military

Professional forces with combined arms. Voluntary but respected service. Defense policy emphasizes deterrence and alliance.
