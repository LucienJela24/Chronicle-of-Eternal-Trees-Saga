Zhonggou Continent

A continent where Eastern and Western cultures merge, creating unique political and social systems

Xi Dynasty Five Kingdoms Union

Overview

The Zhonggou Continent represents a fascinating cultural fusion zone in the Western Plane, where Eastern traditions brought by migrants and traders have merged with indigenous Western practices to create unique political and social systems. The continent's name itself reflects this blending—"Zhonggou" being a Western adaptation of Eastern linguistic elements.

Four major powers dominate the continent: the Xi Dynasty, which maintains Eastern-style imperial governance; the Five Kingdoms, a confederation of smaller states with mixed cultural traditions; the Ming Dynasty, a rival imperial power with distinct cultural characteristics; and the Union, a federation that represents the most successful blending of Eastern and Western political traditions.

The continent's strategic location makes it a crossroads of trade and cultural exchange between the Eastern and Western Planes. This position has brought prosperity but also conflict, as external powers have repeatedly sought to control its resources and influence its politics.

The Four Powers

Xi Dynasty

Eastern Imperial Tradition

The Xi Dynasty maintains the most purely Eastern-style governance on the continent, having been founded by migrants from the Eastern Plane who sought to preserve their traditions in a new land. The Dynasty emphasizes imperial authority, bureaucratic governance, and Confucian social values.

11-Framework Analysis

1. Historical

Founded six centuries ago by refugees from the Eastern Plane who fled political upheaval. The Xi Dynasty has maintained unbroken succession through careful management of imperial bloodlines. Historical records are meticulously preserved.

2. Political

Absolute monarchy with the Emperor as Son of Heaven. The Imperial Court includes six ministries handling administration, revenue, rites, war, justice, and public works. The Censorate monitors officials for corruption.

3. Mental

Education emphasizes classical texts, calligraphy, and civil service examination preparation. The imperial examination system selects officials based on merit rather than birth. Strategic thinking is valued in both civil and military contexts.

4. Spiritual

Ancestor worship is central to spiritual life, with every household maintaining family altars. The Emperor performs rituals to maintain cosmic harmony. Buddhism and Taoism coexist with traditional practices, each with state patronage.

5. Social

Strict social hierarchy with scholars at the top, followed by farmers, artisans, and merchants. Family is the foundation of society, with filial piety as the highest virtue. Women occupy subordinate positions but can wield influence behind the scenes.

6. Cultural

Rich traditions of poetry, painting, and music that emphasize harmony and restraint. Tea ceremonies and seasonal festivals mark the cultural calendar. Imperial patronage supports the arts and preserves ancient traditions.

7. Economical

Agricultural foundation with rice cultivation as the primary crop. State monopolies on salt, iron, and other essentials provide revenue. Trade is regulated but flourishes along rivers and the coast. The imperial treasury is substantial.

8. Physiological

Traditional medicine combining herbal remedies, acupuncture, and energy cultivation. Imperial physicians serve the court and train practitioners. Physical fitness is encouraged through martial arts and calisthenics.

9. Territorial

The largest territory on the continent, encompassing fertile river valleys and mountain ranges. The capital city features the Forbidden Palace and extensive government complexes. Regional capitals administer provinces.

10. Technological

Advanced agricultural techniques support a large population. Engineering projects include canals, dams, and irrigation systems. Gunpowder weapons are manufactured but reserved for military use. Paper, printing, and the compass originated here.

11. Military

Large standing army organized into regional commands. The Imperial Guard protects the capital and the Emperor. Military examinations parallel civil service exams. Naval forces patrol rivers and coastal waters.

Five Kingdoms

Mixed Heritage Confederation

A confederation of five smaller kingdoms, each with its own blend of Eastern and Western cultural elements. The Five Kingdoms cooperate on matters of defense and trade while maintaining distinct identities and internal autonomy.

11-Framework Analysis

1. Historical

The five kingdoms emerged from the fragmentation of a larger state three centuries ago. Rather than continuing warfare, they formed a confederation for mutual benefit. Each kingdom maintains its own history and founding myths.

2. Political

Each kingdom is governed by its own monarch with varying systems—some hereditary, some elective. The Council of Kings coordinates confederation policy. Decisions require consensus, which can slow action but prevents domination by any single kingdom.

3. Mental

Education systems vary by kingdom but generally blend Eastern and Western approaches. Some emphasize classical learning, others practical skills. Cross-border study is encouraged to foster understanding between kingdoms.

4. Spiritual

Religious diversity is the norm, with each kingdom having its own spiritual traditions. Some follow Eastern practices, others Western faiths, and many blend elements of both. Religious tolerance is a confederation principle.

5. Social

Social structures vary between kingdoms, creating interesting contrasts. Intermarriage between kingdoms has created a blended aristocracy. Common people identify with their kingdom first but share a broader confederation identity.

6. Cultural

Each kingdom maintains distinct cultural traditions while participating in confederation-wide festivals. Art, music, and cuisine show both Eastern and Western influences. Cultural exchange is celebrated as a strength.

7. Economical

Free trade between kingdoms is guaranteed by confederation treaty. Each kingdom specializes in different goods—grain, textiles, metals, crafts, and trade goods. Combined, they form a significant economic power.

8. Physiological

Medical practices vary, with some kingdoms favoring Eastern traditional medicine and others Western approaches. Cross-border medical knowledge sharing has improved healthcare throughout the confederation.

9. Territorial

Each kingdom maintains its traditional territory, with clearly defined borders. The central city, neutral ground, hosts confederation institutions. Transportation networks connect all five kingdoms.

10. Technological

Technology sharing between kingdoms accelerates innovation. Some excel in metallurgy, others in textiles or agriculture. Combined expertise makes the confederation technologically competitive with larger states.

11. Military

Each kingdom maintains its own forces but trains to coordinate with allies. The Confederation Guard serves as a rapid reaction force. Combined military strength deters aggression from larger neighbors.

Ming Dynasty

Rival Imperial Power

A rival imperial power that broke away from the Xi Dynasty centuries ago, the Ming Dynasty has developed its own distinct cultural and political identity. While maintaining Eastern-style governance, the Ming have incorporated more Western elements into their society.

11-Framework Analysis

1. Historical

Founded four centuries ago by a rebellious general who established his own imperial line. The Ming have fought several wars with the Xi Dynasty, most ending in stalemate. Each dynasty claims to be the legitimate ruler of all Zhonggou.

2. Political

Imperial system similar to the Xi but with Western-influenced legal reforms. The Emperor shares some power with a Grand Council of advisors. Provincial governors have more autonomy than in the Xi Dynasty.

3. Mental

Education combines Eastern classics with Western sciences and philosophy. The civil service examination includes practical subjects alongside traditional learning. Critical thinking is more encouraged than in the Xi Dynasty.

4. Spiritual

Officially promotes a reformed version of traditional religion that incorporates Western philosophical elements. Religious tolerance is greater than in the Xi Dynasty. The state maintains temples but does not control doctrine.

5. Social

Social hierarchy exists but is less rigid than in the Xi Dynasty. Merchants enjoy higher status due to their economic importance. Women have somewhat more opportunities, including education and property ownership.

6. Cultural

Distinct artistic tradition that blends Eastern aesthetics with Western techniques. Porcelain, silk, and jade work are highly valued. Theater and literature explore themes of individualism and social change.

7. Economical

Commercial economy with active trade both domestically and internationally. Banking and credit systems are well-developed. The Ming actively seek trade relationships with Western Plane powers.

8. Physiological

Combines traditional medicine with Western surgical techniques. Public health measures include sanitation and quarantine protocols. Physical fitness is promoted through both martial arts and Western sports.

9. Territorial

Controls the southern portion of the continent with access to major trade routes. The capital is a cosmopolitan city with significant Western influence. Border fortifications face the Xi Dynasty.

10. Technological

Actively adopts and adapts Western technologies while preserving Eastern innovations. Shipbuilding is particularly advanced. The Ming maintain research institutions that study both Eastern and Western knowledge.

11. Military

Modernized forces combining Eastern and Western military techniques. The navy is particularly strong, protecting trade routes. Firearms and artillery are integrated with traditional forces.

The Union

Federated Republic

The most successful example of East-West political fusion on the continent, the Union combines Eastern-style meritocratic bureaucracy with Western democratic institutions. This hybrid system has created a prosperous and stable state that serves as a model for others.

11-Framework Analysis

1. Historical

Founded two centuries ago by reformers who sought to combine the best of Eastern and Western governance. The Union's constitution is considered a masterpiece of political philosophy. Its success has inspired similar experiments elsewhere.

2. Political

Federal republic with elected legislature and meritocratic civil service. The Chancellor is elected by the Assembly. The Examination Board selects civil servants based on competitive exams. Local governments have significant autonomy.

3. Mental

Education is universal and emphasizes both Eastern and Western knowledge. Critical thinking and civic responsibility are core values. Universities are centers of innovation and debate. Lifelong learning is encouraged.

4. Spiritual

Complete religious freedom with no state religion. All faiths are treated equally under law. Interfaith dialogue is actively promoted. Many citizens follow syncretic beliefs combining Eastern and Western elements.

5. Social

Social mobility is high, with advancement based on merit rather than birth. Equality before the law is guaranteed. Traditional family values coexist with progressive social policies. Community organizations play important roles.

6. Cultural

Vibrant fusion culture celebrated as a unique achievement. Art, music, and literature blend Eastern and Western forms. Cultural institutions receive state support. The Union's cultural exports are popular across the planes.

7. Economical

Mixed economy with both state enterprises and private business. Banking and finance are well-developed. Trade policy is open, with free trade agreements with many partners. Innovation is encouraged and rewarded.

8. Physiological

Universal healthcare combines Eastern and Western medicine. Public health infrastructure is advanced. Physical fitness is promoted through public facilities and programs. Life expectancy is among the highest on the continent.

9. Territorial

Western coastal territory with excellent ports and trade access. The capital is a planned city designed to embody Union principles. Regional centers balance urban and rural development. Infrastructure is well-maintained.

10. Technological

Leader in technological innovation, combining Eastern and Western approaches. Research institutions are well-funded. Patents protect inventors while allowing society to benefit. Magical and mundane technologies are integrated.

11. Military

Professional forces with combined arms and advanced tactics. Military service is voluntary but respected. Defense policy emphasizes deterrence and alliance. The Union prefers diplomacy but maintains credible military strength.
