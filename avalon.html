Overview

The Seven Kingdoms of Avalon represent a unique political entity in the Eastern Plane—a confederation of sovereign monarchies united by ancient treaties, intermarriage, shared magical traditions, and common defense. Named after the legendary island-kingdom from ancient myths, Avalon prides itself on chivalric traditions, courtly culture, and magical scholarship unmatched elsewhere in the realm.

The confederation's origins trace back to the Primordial Era, when the Echo-Born—humans who could hear and channel the World Tree's whispers—gathered in the fertile heartlands of what would become Avalon. These early magic-users established the first academies of the Five Categories: Incantation, Sorcery, Ritual, Armament, and Arcane. Over millennia, their descendants founded the seven kingdoms that now comprise the confederation.

At the center of Avalon's governance stands the rotating High King (or Queen), chosen annually from among the seven monarchs. This system prevents any single kingdom from dominating while ensuring unified leadership for confederation matters. The current tension with the Havenfire Guild—a rising mercantile-magical organization—threatens this carefully balanced system by challenging the traditional magical hierarchy.

11 Frameworks Analysis

1. Historical Framework

Primordial Origins (Abs. 1,300-2,584): Avalon's earliest inhabitants were the Echo-Born—humans who developed the ability to hear and channel the World Tree's whispers during the Primordial Era's magical awakening. These proto-mages gathered in the heartlands where ley lines converged, establishing the first magical communities.

The Five Categories Development (Abs. 1,800-2,200): Echo-Born scholars systematized magic into five distinct categories, each becoming a specialized discipline: Incantation, Sorcery, Ritual, Armament, and Arcane.

The Covenant of Lies (Abs. 2,584): Avalon's ancestors negotiated for the central heartlands, arguing that their magical expertise would be essential for defending against future threats.

Fragmentation into Kingdoms (Year 500-900 / Abs. 3,085-3,485): The ancient Avalonian magical community gradually fragmented into seven distinct realms as different magical philosophies and bloodlines developed territorial identities.

Havenfire Guild Emergence (Year 8,500 / Abs. 11,085): The Havenfire Guild began as a mercantile cooperative but has grown into a major magical power, challenging the traditional dominance of the royal academies.

2. Political Framework

The High King System: Each year, one of the seven monarchs serves as High King (or Queen), providing unified leadership for confederation matters.

  • • Camelot: First month (ceremonial opening)
  • • Lyonesse: Second month
  • • Gorre: Third month
  • • Orcanie: Fourth month
  • • Gaunes: Fifth month
  • • Estregales: Sixth month
  • • Sorelois: Seventh month (ceremonial closing)

The High Council: Comprising the seven monarchs plus representatives from major knightly orders and magical academies. Each kingdom has one vote, with the High King breaking ties.

Magical Aristocracy: Social status is heavily influenced by magical ability. Those showing strong magical talent receive noble titles regardless of birth.

3. Mental Framework

Seven Liberal Arts: Education emphasizes the classical seven liberal arts, augmented by magical theory.

Chivalric Ideals: Knights and nobles are expected to embody courage, honor, courtesy, loyalty, and generosity.

Pathology: Arcane Obsession: The primary psychological risk among Avalon's magic-users is fixation on magical power to the exclusion of all else.

Courtly Love Psychology: The tradition of courtly love shapes emotional expression among the aristocracy, providing inspiration while sometimes creating unrealistic expectations.

4. Spiritual Framework

The Church of the Eternal Light: While less theocratic than Lux Imperio, the Church maintains significant influence, teaching that magic is a divine gift.

World Tree Veneration: Avalonian spirituality centers on the World Tree itself—the source of all magic. Major ceremonies occur at ley line confluences.

The Pendragon Legacy: The ruling dynasty of Camelot claims descent from the Pendragon line, ancient dragon-blooded mages.

Primary Ethereals: Morgana (Wisdom) receives particular veneration as patron of magical scholarship.

5. Social Framework

Feudal Hierarchy with Magical Meritocracy:

  • • Royalty: Reigning monarchs and immediate family
  • • High Nobility: Dukes, counts, and major magical houses
  • • Lesser Nobility: Barons, knights, and minor magical lines
  • • Knightly Class: Warriors with or without magical ability
  • • Magical Commoners: Those with magic but no noble title
  • • Merchants: Growing class, increasingly powerful

Gender Dynamics: Women can hold any position including monarch, knight, and academy master. Inheritance typically follows male-preference primogeniture.

6. Cultural Framework

Bardic Traditions: Bards hold honored positions as keepers of history, performers, and magical practitioners (Incantation category).

Tournaments: Jousting, melee combat, and magical competitions entertain the nobility while demonstrating martial skill.

Magical Arts: Enchanted paintings that move, music that creates visual displays, sculptures that respond to touch.

7. Economical Framework

Currency: Avalonian Crowns (Gold), Kingdom Marks (Silver), Copper Pennies.

Kingdom Specializations: Camelot (magical research), Lyonesse (maritime trade), Gorre (mining/metallurgy), Orcanie (magical components), Gaunes (agriculture), Estregales (military equipment), Sorelois (magical services).

Internal Free Trade: The Treaty of Seven Crowns guarantees free trade between kingdoms, creating a unified market.

8. Physiological Framework

Magical Sensitivity: Approximately 15% of the population shows some magical ability, compared to 5% in other realms.

Lifespan: Average 130 years, with magical practitioners often living longer due to healing magic and vitality enchantments.

Healing Practices: Avalon has the most advanced healing traditions in the Eastern Plane, combining magical healing, herbal medicine, and surgical techniques.

9. Territorial Framework

Geographic Overview: The Seven Kingdoms occupy a contiguous territory in the heart of the Eastern Plane, encompassing rolling hills, ancient forests, coastal regions, and magical ley line confluences.

The Seven Kingdoms:

  • • Camelot: Largest kingdom, political and magical center, home to the rotating High Council seat
  • • Lyonesse: Maritime realm with powerful navy and thriving ports
  • • Gorre: Mountain kingdom rich in minerals and metals, master smiths
  • • Orcanie: Northern reach with strong magical traditions and druidic sites
  • • Gaunes: Verdant agricultural land, Avalon's breadbasket
  • • Estregales: Border march guarding against external threats
  • • Sorelois: Enchanted isle with highest magical academy concentration

The High Citadel (Camelot's Capital): The seat of the High King and the Round Table, built upon a major ley line nexus. Houses the Crystal Spire Academy, the Unwritten Library, and the Wells of Avalon—sacred springs used for the Drowning Test of magical aptitude.

Border Fortresses & Road Networks: A network of fortifications protects Avalon's borders, maintained jointly by all seven kingdoms. The Royal Road runs through all seven capitals, facilitating trade and military movement.

10. Technological Framework

Magical Technology: Communication (message spells, scrying crystals), transportation (portal networks, flying mounts), manufacturing (enchanted tools), defense (wards, animated guardians).

Metallurgy: Gorre's master smiths produce the finest weapons and armor in the Eastern Plane, often with minor enchantments.

Havenfire Innovation: The Guild has developed new magical techniques, particularly in practical applications and mass production, challenging the academies' dominance.

11. Military Framework

Kingdom Forces: Each kingdom maintains its own military (Camelot 40,000, Lyonesse 25,000 naval, Gorre 30,000 heavy infantry, Orcanie 20,000, Gaunes 25,000 militia, Estregales 20,000 border specialists, Sorelois 15,000 magical specialists).

The Avalonian Knights: A pan-kingdom order serving as rapid response forces for confederation defense. Approximately 5,000 knights, requiring noble birth, magical ability, and exceptional martial skill.

Havenfire Military Role: The Guild maintains private security forces that some kingdoms have begun hiring, raising concerns about mercenary dependence and Guild military influence.

Fatal Risk: Havenfire Guild Challenge

The Havenfire Guild's rise represents an existential threat to Avalon's traditional structure:

  • • Political Challenge: Demanding Council representation, undermining royal authority
  • • Economic Challenge: Controlling growing portions of the magical economy
  • • Military Challenge: Maintaining private forces that rival kingdom militias
  • • Cultural Challenge: Promoting meritocratic values that conflict with feudal traditions

The kingdoms must either integrate the Guild into their structure (risking transformation) or suppress it (risking civil conflict). Neither option guarantees Avalon's continued stability.

Key Locations

Camelot

The High Kingdom and largest realm. Contains the Round Table, the University of Avalon, and the rotating seat of the High Council. — See High Citadel map above

University of Avalon

Premier center for magical research and education. Houses the largest library in the Eastern Plane. Currently threatened by Havenfire Guild competition.

Havenfire Guildhall

Sprawling complex serving as the Guild's headquarters. Contains research facilities, training centers, and administrative offices.

The First Echo

Sacred site where the first Echo-Born heard the World Tree's whispers. Major pilgrimage destination and ley line nexus of immense power.

Border Fortresses

Network of fortifications protecting Avalon's borders. Jointly maintained by all seven kingdoms, with garrisons including knights from multiple realms.

Seven Springs

Historic meeting place where the kingdoms' founders gathered. Site of the Treaty of Seven Crowns signing.

Government Structure

Confederation Level

  • High King/Queen: Rotating annual leadership
  • High Council: Seven monarchs plus advisors
  • Avalonian Knights: Pan-kingdom military order
  • University: Confederation-funded research

Kingdom Level

  • Monarch: Hereditary ruler
  • Royal Council: Advisors and ministers
  • Magical Academy: Kingdom-sponsored education
  • Knightly Orders: Kingdom military elite

Local Level

  • Nobles: Regional governance
  • Guilds: Trade regulation and services
  • Magisters: Local magical oversight
  • Local Courts: Justice administration
