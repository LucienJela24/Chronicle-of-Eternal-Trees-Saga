Overview

The Seven Kingdoms of Avalon represent a unique political entity in the Eastern Plane—a confederation of sovereign monarchies united by ancient treaties, intermarriage, shared magical traditions, and common defense. Named after the legendary island-kingdom from ancient myths, Avalon prides itself on chivalric traditions, courtly culture, and magical scholarship unmatched elsewhere in the realm.

The confederation's origins trace back to the Primordial Era, when the Echo-Born—humans who could hear and channel the World Tree's whispers—gathered in the fertile heartlands of what would become Avalon. These early magic-users established the first academies of the Five Categories: Incantation, Sorcery, Ritual, Armament, and Arcane. Over millennia, their descendants founded the seven kingdoms that now comprise the confederation.

At the center of Avalon's governance stands the rotating High King (or Queen), chosen annually from among the seven monarchs. This system prevents any single kingdom from dominating while ensuring unified leadership for confederation matters. The current tension with the Havenfire Guild—a rising mercantile-magical organization—threatens this carefully balanced system by challenging the traditional magical hierarchy.

11 Frameworks Analysis

1. Historical Framework

Primordial Origins (Abs. 1,300-2,584): Avalon's earliest inhabitants were the Echo-Born—humans who developed the ability to hear and channel the World Tree's whispers during the Primordial Era's magical awakening. These proto-mages gathered in the heartlands where ley lines converged, establishing the first magical communities.

The Five Categories Development (Abs. 1,800-2,200): Echo-Born scholars systematized magic into five distinct categories, each becoming a specialized discipline:

  • • Incantation: Spoken magic, words of power
  • • Sorcery: Internal magic, will and visualization
  • • Ritual: Ceremonial magic, symbolic actions
  • • Armament: Enchanted objects and weapons
  • • Arcane: Abstract magic, theoretical and complex

The Covenant of Lies (Abs. 2,584): Avalon's ancestors negotiated for the central heartlands, arguing that their magical expertise would be essential for defending against future threats. The Covenant's signatories agreed, recognizing the strategic value of powerful mages.

Fragmentation into Kingdoms (Year 500-900 / Abs. 3,085-3,485): The ancient Avalonian magical community gradually fragmented into seven distinct realms as different magical philosophies and bloodlines developed territorial identities. This fragmentation was initially seen as a tragedy but eventually proved to create healthy competition and diversity.

Treaty of Seven Crowns (Year 1,000 / Abs. 3,585): The seven kingdoms formally established their confederation, creating the High Council and the rotating High Kingship. This treaty has remained the foundation of Avalonian governance for over 8,500 years.

Havenfire Guild Emergence (Year 8,500 / Abs. 11,085): The Havenfire Guild began as a mercantile cooperative but has grown into a major magical power, challenging the traditional dominance of the royal academies. This represents the most significant internal threat to Avalon's stability in millennia.

2. Political Framework

The High King System: Each year, one of the seven monarchs serves as High King (or Queen), providing unified leadership for confederation matters. The rotation follows a fixed order established by the Treaty of Seven Crowns:

  • • Camelot: First month (ceremonial opening)
  • • Lyonesse: Second month
  • • Gorre: Third month
  • • Orcanie: Fourth month
  • • Gaunes: Fifth month
  • • Estregales: Sixth month
  • • Sorelois: Seventh month (ceremonial closing)

The High Council: Comprising the seven monarchs plus representatives from major knightly orders and magical academies, the Council makes decisions affecting the entire confederation. Each kingdom has one vote, with the High King breaking ties.

Kingdom Sovereignty: Each kingdom maintains:

  • • Independent hereditary monarchy
  • • Own laws and legal system
  • • Own military forces
  • • Control over internal magical education
  • • Right to conduct foreign relations (with Council notification)

Magical Aristocracy: Social status is heavily influenced by magical ability. Those showing strong magical talent receive noble titles regardless of birth, creating a meritocratic element within the feudal structure.

Havenfire Guild Challenge: The Guild has begun fielding candidates for local offices and demanding representation on the High Council, arguing that their economic power and magical innovation entitle them to political voice. This challenge threatens to transform Avalon's political structure.

3. Mental Framework

Seven Liberal Arts: Education in Avalon emphasizes the classical seven liberal arts, augmented by magical theory:

  • • Trivium: Grammar, Logic, Rhetoric
  • • Quadrivium: Arithmetic, Geometry, Music, Astronomy
  • • Magica: Theory and practice of the Five Categories

Chivalric Ideals: Knights and nobles are expected to embody chivalric virtues: courage, honor, courtesy, loyalty, and generosity. These ideals shape mental frameworks across social classes, with even commoners aspiring to chivalric behavior.

Pathology: Arcane Obsession: The primary psychological risk among Avalon's magic-users is fixation on magical power to the exclusion of all else. This manifests as social withdrawal, ethical compromise, and eventually magical corruption. The academies provide counseling, but cases persist.

Critical Thinking Tradition: Unlike some realms that discourage questioning, Avalon's academies encourage debate and intellectual challenge. The famous "Avalonian Method" involves testing magical theories through rigorous experimentation and peer review.

Courtly Love Psychology: The tradition of courtly love—idealized, often unrequited romantic devotion—shapes emotional expression among the aristocracy. This can be psychologically complex, providing inspiration while sometimes creating unrealistic expectations.

4. Spiritual Framework

The Church of the Eternal Light: While Avalon is less theocratic than Lux Imperio, the Church maintains significant influence. It teaches that magic is a divine gift to be used responsibly, and that the World Tree's whispers are messages from the divine.

World Tree Veneration: Unlike other realms that worship specific Ethereals, Avalonian spirituality centers on the World Tree itself—the source of all magic. Major ceremonies occur at sites where ley lines converge, believed to be points where the Tree's presence is strongest.

The Pendragon Legacy: The ruling dynasty of Camelot claims descent from the Pendragon line, ancient dragon-blooded mages who were among the first Echo-Born. This lineage is considered spiritually significant, though other kingdoms dispute its unique importance.

Magical Ethics: Each of the Five Categories has associated ethical codes. Violations—using Incantation for deception, Sorcery for domination, Ritual for desecration, Armament for unjust war, or Arcane for reality-breaking—are considered spiritual failings as well as crimes.

Pilgrimage Traditions: Major pilgrimage sites include the First Echo (where the first Echo-Born heard the Tree), the Seven Springs (where the kingdoms' founders met), and various ley line confluences. Pilgrimages are considered spiritually cleansing regardless of social status.

Primary Ethereals: Morgana (Wisdom) receives particular veneration as patron of magical scholarship. Other widely worshipped Ethereals include those associated with knowledge, craft, and protection.

5. Social Framework

Feudal Hierarchy with Magical Meritocracy:

  • • Royalty: Reigning monarchs and immediate family
  • • High Nobility: Dukes, counts, and major magical houses
  • • Lesser Nobility: Barons, knights, and minor magical lines
  • • Knightly Class: Warriors with or without magical ability
  • • Magical Commoners: Those with magic but no noble title
  • • Merchants: Growing class, increasingly powerful
  • • Artisans and Peasants: Traditional commoner classes

Social Mobility: Unlike more rigid feudal systems, Avalon permits significant movement between classes:

  • • Magical awakening can elevate commoners to nobility
  • • Knightly service can grant commoners knighthood
  • • Commercial success can purchase noble titles (controversial)
  • • Academic achievement can lead to advisory positions

Intermarriage Politics: Marriages between kingdoms are carefully negotiated to strengthen political bonds. The children of such unions often have complex loyalties, serving as bridges between realms.

Gender Dynamics: Women can hold any position including monarch, knight, and academy master. However, inheritance typically follows male-preference primogeniture, creating occasional succession disputes.

Havenfire Guild Impact: The Guild's rise has created a new social category—magical professionals who owe loyalty to their employer rather than their kingdom. This challenges traditional feudal obligations.

6. Cultural Framework

Bardic Traditions: Bards hold honored positions as keepers of history, performers, and magical practitioners (Incantation category). The greatest bards can weave magic into their performances, influencing emotions and even reality through song and story.

Courtly Culture: Each kingdom maintains elaborate courtly traditions including:

  • • Poetry competitions: Testing wit and romantic expression
  • • Musical performances: Both magical and mundane
  • • Illuminated manuscripts: Combining art, calligraphy, and minor enchantments
  • • Courtly love rituals: Formalized romantic expression

Tournaments: Jousting, melee combat, and magical competitions entertain the nobility while demonstrating martial skill. The Grand Tournament, held annually at rotating locations, draws participants from all seven kingdoms.

Arthurian Legacy: The legend of King Arthur and the Knights of the Round Table serves as cultural inspiration. While historians debate Arthur's historical existence, his story shapes Avalonian ideals of unity, chivalry, and noble sacrifice.

Magical Arts: Beyond practical magic, Avalon has developed magical art forms—enchanted paintings that move, music that creates visual displays, sculptures that respond to touch. Master magical artists are celebrated throughout the confederation.

Cuisine: Regional cuisines vary by kingdom, with magical ingredients and preparation methods creating unique dishes. Feast culture is important, with elaborate meals serving social and diplomatic functions.

7. Economical Framework

Currency System:

  • • Avalonian Crowns (Gold): High-value currency accepted across all seven kingdoms
  • • Kingdom Marks (Silver): Kingdom-specific currency, exchangeable at fixed rates
  • • Copper Pennies: Standard small transactions

Kingdom Specializations:

  • • Camelot: Magical research, education, high-value enchantments
  • • Lyonesse: Maritime trade, shipbuilding, naval services
  • • Gorre: Mining, metallurgy, weapon and armor production
  • • Orcanie: Magical components, rare herbs, mystical artifacts
  • • Gaunes: Agriculture, wine production, food exports
  • • Estregales: Military equipment, border defense services
  • • Sorelois: Magical services, divination, arcane consultation

Internal Free Trade: The Treaty of Seven Crowns guarantees free trade between kingdoms, creating a unified market larger than any single kingdom's economy. This has been a major factor in Avalon's prosperity.

Guild System: Craft and merchant guilds regulate trades, maintain quality standards, and provide social services. The Havenfire Guild began as a merchant guild before expanding into magical services.

Magical Economy: A significant portion of Avalon's economy involves magical goods and services—enchanted items, magical healing, divination, and education. This magical economy gives Avalon unique advantages in inter-realm trade.

8. Physiological Framework

Magical Sensitivity: Avalonians show higher rates of magical sensitivity than populations elsewhere, likely due to centuries of selective pressure and ley line exposure. Approximately 15% of the population shows some magical ability, compared to 5% in other realms.

Lifespan: Average lifespan is 130 years, with magical practitioners often living longer due to healing magic and vitality enchantments. The longest-lived Avalonians are typically Arcane specialists who develop longevity rituals.

Healing Practices: Avalon has the most advanced healing traditions in the Eastern Plane, combining:

  • • Magical healing: Direct magical intervention for injuries and disease
  • • Herbal medicine: Sophisticated pharmacology using magical and mundane plants
  • • Surgical techniques: Advanced for the setting, aided by magical anesthesia
  • • Preventive care: Vitality enchantments and health monitoring

Magical Exhaustion: Common condition among magic-users who overextend their abilities. Symptoms include headache, nosebleeds, unconsciousness, and in severe cases, magical burnout (permanent loss of abilities).

Physical Training: Knights and warriors maintain rigorous fitness regimens. Even magic-users are encouraged to maintain physical health, as bodily condition affects magical capacity.

Dietary Practices: Magical nutrition supplements traditional diet. Enchanted foods provide enhanced nutrition, while alchemical preparations address specific health needs.

9. Territorial Framework

Geographic Overview: The Seven Kingdoms occupy a contiguous territory in the heart of the Eastern Plane, encompassing:

  • • Rolling hills and fertile valleys: Agricultural heartland
  • • Ancient forests: Magical wood sources and sacred sites
  • • Coastal regions: Maritime access (primarily Lyonesse)
  • • Magical ley line confluences: Powering the realm's magic

The Seven Kingdoms:

  • • Camelot: Largest kingdom, political and magical center, home to the rotating High Council seat
  • • Lyonesse: Maritime realm with powerful navy and thriving ports
  • • Gorre: Mountain kingdom rich in minerals and metals, master smiths
  • • Orcanie: Northern reach with strong magical traditions and druidic sites
  • • Gaunes: Verdant agricultural land, Avalon's breadbasket
  • • Estregales: Border march guarding against external threats
  • • Sorelois: Enchanted isle with highest magical academy concentration

Border Fortresses: A network of fortifications protects Avalon's borders, maintained jointly by all seven kingdoms. The fortress garrisons include knights from multiple kingdoms, fostering inter-kingdom cooperation.

Road Networks: Well-maintained roads connect all seven kingdoms, facilitating trade, communication, and military movement. The Royal Road runs through all seven capitals.

10. Technological Framework

Magical Technology: Avalon's "technology" is primarily magical rather than mechanical:

  • • Communication: Message spells, scrying crystals, enchanted mirrors
  • • Transportation: Portal networks (limited), flying mounts, levitation enchantments
  • • Manufacturing: Enchanted tools, animated assistants, magical forging
  • • Defense: Wards, magical barriers, animated guardians

Metallurgy: Gorre's master smiths produce the finest weapons and armor in the Eastern Plane, often with minor enchantments. Avalonian steel is prized throughout the realms.

Magical Research: The University of Avalon (in Camelot) serves as the premier center for magical research. Current projects include:

  • • Long-distance communication improvements
  • • Healing magic advancement
  • • Defensive ward enhancement
  • • Theoretical Arcane studies

Architectural Engineering: Magical construction techniques allow impressive structures—floating towers, self-maintaining fortresses, buildings larger than mundane engineering would permit.

Havenfire Innovation: The Guild has developed new magical techniques, particularly in practical applications and mass production. Their innovations challenge the academies' traditional dominance in magical research.

11. Military Framework

Kingdom Forces: Each kingdom maintains its own military:

  • • Camelot: 40,000 (largest force, includes magical elite)
  • • Lyonesse: 25,000 (primarily naval)
  • • Gorre: 30,000 (heavy infantry specialists)
  • • Orcanie: 20,000 (includes druidic warriors)
  • • Gaunes: 25,000 (primarily militia)
  • • Estregales: 20,000 (border specialists)
  • • Sorelois: 15,000 (magical specialists)

The Avalonian Knights: A pan-kingdom order serving as rapid response forces for confederation defense. Membership is highly prestigious, requiring noble birth, magical ability, and exceptional martial skill. Approximately 5,000 knights serve at any time.

Knightly Orders: Beyond the Avalonian Knights, each kingdom maintains specialized orders:

  • • Order of the Round Table: Camelot's elite, bodyguards to the High King
  • • Sea Guard: Lyonesse's naval knights
  • • Stone Sentinels: Gorre's fortress defenders
  • • Wild Hunt: Orcanie's druidic cavalry

Military Organization: Feudal levies supplemented by professional soldiers. Knights form the elite core, supported by men-at-arms, archers, and magical support. Combined arms tactics integrate heavy cavalry, infantry, archers, and battle-mages.

Havenfire Military Role: The Guild maintains private security forces that some kingdoms have begun hiring, raising concerns about mercenary dependence and Guild military influence.

Fatal Risk: Havenfire Guild Challenge

The Havenfire Guild's rise represents an existential threat to Avalon's traditional structure:

  • • Political Challenge: Demanding Council representation, undermining royal authority
  • • Economic Challenge: Controlling growing portions of the magical economy
  • • Military Challenge: Maintaining private forces that rival kingdom militias
  • • Cultural Challenge: Promoting meritocratic values that conflict with feudal traditions

If the Guild continues growing unchecked, it could:

  • • Transform Avalon from a confederation of monarchies into a mercantile oligarchy
  • • Destroy the magical academy system that has preserved knowledge for millennia
  • • Create internal conflict as kingdoms take sides in the dispute
  • • Weaken Avalon's ability to respond to external threats like the Abyss

The kingdoms must either integrate the Guild into their structure (risking transformation) or suppress it (risking civil conflict). Neither option guarantees Avalon's continued stability.

Key Locations

Camelot

The High Kingdom and largest realm. Contains the Round Table, the University of Avalon, and the rotating seat of the High Council. Political and magical center of the confederation.

University of Avalon

Premier center for magical research and education. Houses the largest library in the Eastern Plane and trains the most powerful mages. Currently threatened by Havenfire Guild competition.

Havenfire Guildhall

Sprawling complex serving as the Guild's headquarters. Contains research facilities, training centers, and administrative offices. Symbol of the challenge to traditional magical authority.

The First Echo

Sacred site where the first Echo-Born heard the World Tree's whispers. Major pilgrimage destination and ley line nexus of immense power.

Border Fortresses

Network of fortifications protecting Avalon's borders. Jointly maintained by all seven kingdoms, with garrisons including knights from multiple realms.

Seven Springs

Historic meeting place where the kingdoms' founders gathered. Site of the Treaty of Seven Crowns signing. Continuing location for major diplomatic ceremonies.

Government Structure

Confederation Level

  • High King/Queen: Rotating annual leadership
  • High Council: Seven monarchs plus advisors
  • Avalonian Knights: Pan-kingdom military order
  • University: Confederation-funded research

Kingdom Level

  • Monarch: Hereditary ruler
  • Royal Council: Advisors and ministers
  • Magical Academy: Kingdom-sponsored education
  • Knightly Orders: Kingdom military elite

Local Level

  • Nobles: Regional governance
  • Guilds: Trade regulation and services
  • Magisters: Local magical oversight
  • Local Courts: Justice administration
