The Passage and the Dwelling

The term "Meridian" encompasses two distinct but connected aspects of the cosmology: the Meridian Threshold — the primary sealing point between the Eastern and Western Planes, anchored by Gallian, the First Guardian — and the Meridian Domain, the hidden space between the Two Trees where the Fourth Lord and the Overseers dwell. The Threshold is the passage; the Domain is the dwelling.

Together, they form the axis of relation between the planes — the liminal space that prevents the Tree of Order and the Tree of Chaos from consuming each other prematurely. It is here that the Old Tree, last remnant of the Wild of Aletheia, stands as eternal witness.

The Meridian Threshold

Located at the exact boundary between the Eastern and Western Planes, the Meridian Threshold is the primary sealing point against the Void and the Grenlian incursions. During the War of the Grenlians (Book 1.5), Gallian, the First Guardian and threshold-keeper, sacrificed his physical form to become the living anchor of the seal. His essence now maintains the barrier, whispering across the ages: "The center holds."

The Threshold is also the site of the final sealing of the Dark Emperors (Year 7003, 2nd Myriad) and the location where the Essence of WILL was later distributed to the Twelve Watchers. It is the most heavily guarded liminal point in the known cosmos.

The Meridian Domain

Beyond the Threshold lies the Meridian Domain — the Sanctuary of the Fourth Lord. This is the space between the Two Trees, where the Blood-River flows and the Old Tree grows. It is not a place of judgment or transit, but of preservation: the Fourth Lord and the Overseers guard the knowledge that must remain hidden until the Convergence.

The Domain is accessible only through the Nexus of Ways in the Great Forest, and only to those who have completed the Wanderer's Path. It was here that Lucius Arogzan met the Fourth Lord and received the revelation of the Eight Nodes.

The Fourth Lord & The Overseers

The Fourth Persona

The Fourth Lord is the Persona of Mystery, Magic, and Possibility. Hidden since the First Myriad, she revealed herself only to those who have walked the Wanderer's Path. Her function is to preserve the unknown — the capacity for wonder that makes knowledge meaningful. She appears as a shifting form of light and shadow, casting no shadow of her own.

"The Fourth Lord does not rule. She waits."

The Overseers

Servants of the Fourth Persona, the Overseers are witness-preservers — consciousness-aspects that record all knowledge across the planes while keeping dangerous secrets hidden. Key figures include Elder Overseer Myrddin (Keeper of the Sanctuary's secrets) and Overseer Seren (advocate for measured revelation). They stepped further out of concealment after Lucius completed the Wanderer's Path.

The Old Tree & The Blood-River

Within the Meridian Domain stands the Old Tree — the last remnant of the Wild of Aletheia, preserved from the genocide of the Observers. It is the boundary-wood, the witness that remembers what the Two Trees have forgotten. Tended by Lucien Jela (Fourth Librarian) in his eternal retirement, it serves as the living bridge between Order and Chaos.

Flowing between the roots of the Two Trees is the Blood-River, formed from the sacrificial essence of the Earlier Gods. It separates the Trees so they do not consume each other, nourishes both planes, and carries the memory of divine sacrifice. The river is the medium of all cross-planar travel.

The Eighth Clue & The Gate

In Book 4, Lucius Arogzan reached the Nexus of Ways in the Great Forest after collecting seven clues across the planes. The eighth clue was not an object but the gate itself — requiring the seven collected clues to open. When placed, the gate revealed the path to the Meridian Domain and the Sanctuary of the Fourth Lord.

This moment marked the culmination of the Wanderer's Path: Lucius had become the center, the space between, the relation that fills vacuums. The Fourth Lord then revealed to him the true nature of his journey and the coming Convergence.
