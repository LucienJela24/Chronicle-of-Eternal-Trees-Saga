Western Plane Five Powers

Central Continent

A continent divided among five major powers, each vying for dominance and survival

Aurelius Empire Legion Republic

Overview

The Central Continent of the Western Plane is home to five major powers that have shaped the history and politics of the region for centuries. These nations—the Aurelius Empire, the Legion, the Republic, the Neo-Imperium, and the Dominion Republic—represent diverse approaches to governance, from absolute monarchy to military dictatorship to democratic republicanism.

Constant tension and periodic warfare between these powers have defined Central Continent politics. Alliances shift with the wind, and today's friend may become tomorrow's enemy. The presence of Abyssal threats has occasionally forced cooperation, but old rivalries die hard.

Despite their conflicts, the Central Continent powers share a common cultural heritage rooted in the ancient civilization that once unified the region. Latin remains a lingua franca among the educated classes, and philosophical and artistic traditions cross political boundaries.

The Five Powers

Aurelius Empire

The Imperial Tradition

The oldest and largest of the Central Continent powers, the Aurelius Empire claims descent from the ancient civilization that once ruled the entire continent. Governed by the Aurelius dynasty, the Empire maintains traditions of imperial governance, patronage of the arts, and military expansion.

11-Framework Analysis

1. Historical

Founded over a thousand years ago by Aurelius the Great, who unified the warring tribes of the continent. The Empire claims direct descent from the ancient civilization, preserving its laws, customs, and traditions through the centuries.

2. Political

Absolute monarchy with the Emperor holding supreme authority. The Senate advises on policy but holds no binding power. Provincial governors administer regions on behalf of the throne. Succession follows primogeniture with occasional civil wars.

3. Mental

Education emphasizes classical learning, rhetoric, and philosophy. The elite are trained to think strategically and administratively. Commoners receive basic education focused on practical skills and loyalty to the Empire.

4. Spiritual

The Imperial Cult venerates the Emperor as the divine representative of the gods. Traditional pantheon worship remains strong, with temples receiving state support. Religious tolerance is practiced, but the Imperial Cult holds privileged status.

5. Social

Rigid class hierarchy with the imperial family at the apex, followed by patricians, plebeians, and slaves. Social mobility is limited but possible through military service or imperial favor. Family connections and patronage networks determine advancement.

6. Cultural

Rich tradition of art, literature, and architecture that draws from ancient models. The Empire patronizes artists and scholars to glorify its history. Gladiatorial games and public spectacles entertain the masses and demonstrate imperial power.

7. Economical

Agricultural foundation with extensive trade networks. The Empire mints the most widely accepted currency in the Western Plane. Taxation is heavy but funds public works, military campaigns, and the imperial court.

8. Physiological

Advanced medical knowledge preserved from ancient times. Public baths and sanitation systems promote health. The elite enjoy the best care, while commoners rely on traditional remedies. Military training emphasizes physical fitness.

9. Territorial

The largest territory on the Central Continent, encompassing diverse climates and resources. The capital city is one of the world's greatest metropolises. Extensive road networks connect provincial centers to the heartland.

10. Technological

Preserves and maintains ancient technologies, including aqueducts, roads, and siege engines. Engineering corps are highly skilled. Magical research is conducted under imperial supervision, with breakthroughs reserved for state use.

11. Military

Professional legions form the backbone of imperial power. Each legion is a self-contained fighting force with infantry, cavalry, and support units. The Praetorian Guard protects the Emperor and serves as an elite reserve.

The Legion

Military Meritocracy

A nation built around its military, where citizenship is earned through service and social status derives from military achievement. The Legion's disciplined armies are feared throughout the Western Plane, and its commanders are among the finest strategists in existence.

11-Framework Analysis

1. Historical

Originated as a mercenary company that carved out its own territory during the collapse of the previous empire. Over generations, the Legion transformed from a band of soldiers into a nation-state governed by military principles.

2. Political

Military oligarchy led by the High Command, composed of the most senior and accomplished officers. Citizenship is earned through military service. Non-citizens are subject to military law but cannot vote or hold office.

3. Mental

Education is entirely focused on military training and discipline from early childhood. Critical thinking is encouraged within tactical and strategic contexts. Loyalty, duty, and sacrifice are the highest virtues instilled in all citizens.

4. Spiritual

The Legion honors the spirits of fallen comrades and worships war deities. Religious services are conducted as military ceremonies. Chaplains serve alongside combat units, providing spiritual guidance and maintaining morale.

5. Social

Social hierarchy is determined entirely by military rank and accomplishment. Family lineage means nothing compared to personal achievement. Veterans are highly respected and often become community leaders after retirement.

6. Cultural

Culture revolves around military traditions—drill, ceremony, and commemoration of battles. Music consists of marching songs and martial anthems. Art typically depicts heroic military scenes and honors fallen heroes.

7. Economical

War economy with state-controlled production of weapons, armor, and supplies. Mercenary contracts provide significant income. Agriculture and basic industries support the population, but military spending dominates the budget.

8. Physiological

Physical fitness is mandatory for all citizens. Military training begins in childhood and continues throughout life. Medical care is excellent for active service members but limited for non-citizens and the elderly.

9. Territorial

Compact territory organized as a fortified camp on a continental scale. The capital is essentially a massive military base. Border regions are heavily fortified with walls, watchtowers, and garrison towns.

10. Technological

Military technology is highly advanced, including siege engines, armored vehicles, and communication systems. Innovation is encouraged but must serve military purposes. Engineers are valued members of society.

11. Military

The entire society is organized for war. Every citizen is a soldier. The Legion's forces are renowned for discipline, tactical flexibility, and endurance. Specialized units include heavy infantry, cavalry, engineers, and battle-mages.

The Republic

Democratic Governance

A democratic republic where citizens elect representatives to govern on their behalf. The Republic prides itself on its freedoms, its legal system, and its commercial success. While not the largest or most powerful of the five, it is often the most prosperous and innovative.

11-Framework Analysis

1. Historical

Founded by revolutionaries who overthrew a tyrannical king two centuries ago. The Republic's constitution enshrines principles of liberty, equality, and rule of law. Its history is marked by struggles to expand citizenship and protect freedoms.

2. Political

Representative democracy with a bicameral legislature elected by citizens. The Consul serves as head of state and government, elected for limited terms. An independent judiciary interprets the constitution. Local governments have significant autonomy.

3. Mental

Education emphasizes critical thinking, civic responsibility, and practical skills. Public schools are available to all citizens. Universities encourage innovation and debate. The press is free and plays a vital role in public discourse.

4. Spiritual

Complete separation of church and state. All faiths are tolerated, and none receive state support. Many citizens follow traditional religions, while others embrace philosophical schools or secular humanism.

5. Social

Social mobility is high compared to neighboring states. Birth matters less than talent and effort. A growing middle class dominates commerce and professions. Debates over expanding citizenship to non-humans and former slaves continue.

6. Cultural

Vibrant cultural scene with theater, music, and literature flourishing. Artists enjoy patronage from wealthy merchants and public support. The Republic's culture emphasizes individual expression and challenges to tradition.

7. Economical

Market economy with strong commercial traditions. Banking and trade are major industries. Guilds regulate crafts and professions. The Republic's currency is trusted throughout the Western Plane, and its merchants are found everywhere.

8. Physiological

Medical care is widely available, with both public clinics and private practitioners. Sanitation and public health measures are advanced. Physical fitness is encouraged but not mandatory. Life expectancy is higher than in neighboring states.

9. Territorial

Moderate territory with excellent ports and trade routes. The capital is a bustling commercial center. Towns and cities enjoy self-governance under republican principles. Border regions are defended by citizen militias and professional forces.

10. Technological

Innovation is encouraged and rewarded. Inventors can patent their creations and profit from them. The Republic leads in areas like navigation, printing, and manufacturing. Magical research is conducted by private academies.

11. Military

Citizen militia forms the backbone of defense, supplemented by professional units. Military service is voluntary but highly respected. The Republic's forces excel in naval warfare and defensive operations rather than aggressive campaigns.

Neo-Imperium

Revived Imperial Ambition

A relatively new power founded by a charismatic leader who claimed to restore the ancient empire's glory. The Neo-Imperium combines elements of imperial tradition with revolutionary ideology, creating a dynamic and expansionist state that threatens its neighbors.

11-Framework Analysis

1. Historical

Founded fifty years ago by the Supreme Leader, who united warring factions under a vision of restored glory. The Neo-Imperium has rapidly expanded through conquest and subversion, claiming to be the true heir of ancient civilization.

2. Political

Authoritarian state with the Supreme Leader holding absolute power. A cult of personality surrounds the Leader, whose word is law. The Party apparatus manages day-to-day governance and ensures ideological conformity.

3. Mental

Education is strictly controlled and serves state propaganda. Critical thinking is discouraged outside approved channels. Citizens are taught to venerate the Supreme Leader and the state's ideology. Independent thought is dangerous.

4. Spiritual

The Supreme Leader is venerated as a semi-divine figure. Traditional religions are suppressed or co-opted. State ideology functions as a secular religion, with rituals, symbols, and sacred texts glorifying the Leader and the state.

5. Social

Social hierarchy is based on loyalty to the regime and service to the state. Party members enjoy privileges denied to ordinary citizens. Dissidents are sent to re-education camps or labor colonies. Fear and surveillance maintain control.

6. Cultural

State-controlled culture glorifies the Supreme Leader and the Neo-Imperium's achievements. Art, music, and literature must serve ideological purposes. Historical revisionism reconstructs the past to justify present policies.

7. Economical

Command economy directed by the state. Private enterprise is limited and closely regulated. Resources are directed toward military expansion and monumental projects. Shortages are common, but propaganda maintains morale.

8. Physiological

Medical care is adequate for Party members and the military but limited for others. Physical fitness is promoted as preparation for national service. Eugenic programs aim to improve the population according to state ideology.

9. Territorial

Rapidly expanding territory through conquest and annexation. The capital features monumental architecture glorifying the regime. Newly conquered regions are integrated through colonization and cultural assimilation programs.

10. Technological

Technology is prioritized for military applications. Espionage and theft of foreign innovations are common. The state invests heavily in weapons research, including dangerous magical experiments forbidden elsewhere.

11. Military

Massive military fueled by conscription and ideological fervor. The Neo-Imperium's forces are numerous if not always well-equipped. Fanatical loyalty to the Supreme Leader makes them dangerous opponents. Special units include battle-mages and terror troops.

Dominion Republic

Federal Alliance

A federal union of smaller states that banded together for mutual protection and prosperity. The Dominion Republic operates on principles of shared sovereignty, with member states retaining significant autonomy while cooperating on matters of defense, trade, and foreign policy.

11-Framework Analysis

1. Historical

Formed a century ago when seven small states united to resist absorption by larger neighbors. The federal compact has been expanded twice to include additional states. The Dominion Republic's history demonstrates the viability of cooperative governance.

2. Political

Federal system with power divided between central government and member states. The Federal Assembly represents the people, while the Council of States ensures member state interests. The Chancellor serves as head of government.

3. Mental

Education varies by member state but emphasizes civic responsibility and critical thinking. The federal government supports universities and research institutions. Citizens are expected to participate in both state and federal governance.

4. Spiritual

Religious freedom is guaranteed, and member states have diverse spiritual traditions. No federal religious establishment exists. Interfaith dialogue is encouraged as part of building federal unity.

5. Social

Social structures vary between member states, creating a diverse tapestry. Federal citizenship coexists with state identities. Free movement between states allows social mixing and prevents isolation.

6. Cultural

Rich cultural diversity is celebrated as a strength. Federal programs support cultural exchange between states. A shared identity as Dominion citizens coexists with state and local loyalties.

7. Economical

Common market allows free trade between member states. Federal currency supplements state currencies. Economic development programs assist less prosperous regions. Combined, the Dominion Republic has significant economic weight.

8. Physiological

Health standards are coordinated at the federal level. Medical research is shared between states. Public health emergencies are addressed through federal coordination. Life expectancy varies but is generally improving.

9. Territorial

Member states retain their traditional territories. Federal districts house central institutions. Border disputes are resolved through federal arbitration. Infrastructure projects connect member states and promote integration.

10. Technological

Research collaboration between states accelerates innovation. Federal standards ensure compatibility of systems. Member states specialize in different technological areas and share expertise.

11. Military

Federal Defense Force combines units from all member states. Each state maintains its own militia for internal security. Coordination through federal command structure ensures unified response to external threats.
