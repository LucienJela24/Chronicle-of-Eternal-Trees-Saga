Overview

The Central Continent of the Western Plane is home to five major powers that have shaped the history and politics of the region for centuries. These nations—the Aurelius Empire, the Legion, the Republic, the Neo-Imperium, and the Dominion Republic—represent diverse approaches to governance, from absolute monarchy to military dictatorship to democratic republicanism.

Constant tension and periodic warfare between these powers have defined Central Continent politics. Alliances shift with the wind, and today's friend may become tomorrow's enemy. The presence of Abyssal threats has occasionally forced cooperation, but old rivalries die hard.

Despite their conflicts, the Central Continent powers share a common cultural heritage rooted in the ancient civilization that once unified the region. Latin remains a lingua franca among the educated classes, and philosophical and artistic traditions cross political boundaries.

The Five Powers

Aurelius Empire

The Imperial Tradition

The oldest and largest of the Central Continent powers, the Aurelius Empire claims descent from the ancient civilization that once ruled the entire continent. Governed by the Aurelius dynasty, the Empire maintains traditions of imperial governance, patronage of the arts, and military expansion.

11-Framework Analysis

1. Historical

Founded over a thousand years ago by Aurelius the Great, who unified the warring tribes of the continent. The Empire claims direct descent from the ancient civilization.

2. Political

Absolute monarchy with the Emperor holding supreme authority. The Senate advises but holds no binding power. Provincial governors administer regions.

3. Mental

Education emphasizes classical learning, rhetoric, and philosophy. The elite are trained to think strategically and administratively.

4. Spiritual

The Imperial Cult venerates the Emperor as divine representative. Traditional pantheon worship remains strong with state-supported temples.

5. Social

Rigid class hierarchy: imperial family, patricians, plebeians, slaves. Social mobility limited but possible through military service.

6. Cultural

Rich tradition of art, literature, and architecture. Gladiatorial games and public spectacles entertain the masses.

7. Economical

Agricultural foundation with extensive trade networks. The Empire mints the most widely accepted currency in the Western Plane.

8. Physiological

Advanced medical knowledge preserved from ancient times. Public baths and sanitation systems promote health.

9. Territorial

Largest territory on the Central Continent. Extensive road networks connect provincial centers to the heartland.

10. Technological

Preserves ancient technologies: aqueducts, roads, siege engines. Magical research conducted under imperial supervision.

11. Military

Professional legions form the backbone. Each legion is self-contained. Praetorian Guard protects the Emperor.

The Legion

Military Meritocracy

A nation built around its military, where citizenship is earned through service and social status derives from military achievement. The Legion's disciplined armies are feared throughout the Western Plane.

11-Framework Analysis

1. Historical

Originated as a mercenary company that carved out territory during empire collapse. Transformed into a nation-state governed by military principles.

2. Political

Military oligarchy led by High Command. Citizenship earned through service. Non-citizens subject to military law.

3. Mental

Education focused on military training from childhood. Critical thinking within tactical contexts. Loyalty, duty, sacrifice are highest virtues.

4. Spiritual

Honors fallen comrades and war deities. Chaplains serve alongside combat units providing spiritual guidance.

5. Social

Hierarchy determined by military rank and accomplishment. Family lineage means nothing compared to personal achievement.

6. Cultural

Culture revolves around military traditions—drill, ceremony, battle commemoration. Music consists of marching songs and martial anthems.

7. Economical

War economy with state-controlled production. Mercenary contracts provide significant income. Military spending dominates budget.

8. Physiological

Physical fitness mandatory for all citizens. Training begins in childhood. Medical care excellent for active service members.

9. Territorial

Compact territory organized as fortified camp. Capital is essentially massive military base. Border regions heavily fortified.

10. Technological

Military technology highly advanced. Innovation encouraged but must serve military purposes. Engineers valued members of society.

11. Military

Entire society organized for war. Every citizen a soldier. Renowned for discipline, tactical flexibility, and endurance.

The Republic

Democratic Governance

A democratic republic where citizens elect representatives to govern. The Republic prides itself on freedoms, legal systems, and commercial success—often the most prosperous and innovative power.

11-Framework Analysis

1. Historical

Founded by revolutionaries who overthrew a tyrannical king. Constitution enshrines liberty, equality, and rule of law.

2. Political

Representative democracy with bicameral legislature. Consul serves as head of state. Independent judiciary interprets constitution.

3. Mental

Education emphasizes critical thinking and civic responsibility. Public schools available to all citizens. Free press plays vital role.

4. Spiritual

Complete separation of church and state. All faiths tolerated. Many follow traditional religions or philosophical schools.

5. Social

High social mobility. Birth matters less than talent. Growing middle class dominates commerce. Debates over expanding citizenship continue.

6. Cultural

Vibrant cultural scene with theater, music, and literature. Artists enjoy patronage from wealthy merchants and public support.

7. Economical

Market economy with strong commercial traditions. Banking and trade are major industries. Currency trusted throughout Western Plane.

8. Physiological

Medical care widely available. Sanitation and public health advanced. Life expectancy higher than neighboring states.

9. Territorial

Moderate territory with excellent ports and trade routes. Capital is bustling commercial center. Towns enjoy self-governance.

10. Technological

Innovation encouraged and rewarded. Leads in navigation, printing, and manufacturing. Magical research by private academies.

11. Military

Citizen militia forms backbone, supplemented by professionals. Excel in naval warfare and defensive operations.

Neo-Imperium

Revived Imperial Ambition

A relatively new power founded by a charismatic leader claiming to restore ancient glory. Combines imperial tradition with revolutionary ideology, creating a dynamic and expansionist state threatening its neighbors.

11-Framework Analysis

1. Historical

Founded fifty years ago by the Supreme Leader. Rapidly expanded through conquest and subversion.

2. Political

Authoritarian state with Supreme Leader holding absolute power. Cult of personality surrounds the Leader.

3. Mental

Education strictly controlled serving state propaganda. Critical thinking discouraged outside approved channels.

4. Spiritual

Supreme Leader venerated as semi-divine. Traditional religions suppressed or co-opted. State ideology functions as secular religion.

5. Social

Hierarchy based on loyalty to regime. Party members enjoy privileges. Dissidents sent to re-education camps.

6. Cultural

State-controlled culture glorifying the Leader. Art, music, literature must serve ideological purposes.

7. Economical

Command economy directed by state. Resources directed toward military expansion and monumental projects.

8. Physiological

Medical care adequate for Party members and military. Eugenic programs aim to improve population.

9. Territorial

Rapidly expanding through conquest. Capital features monumental architecture glorifying the regime.

10. Technological

Technology prioritized for military. Espionage common. Heavy investment in weapons research including dangerous experiments.

11. Military

Massive military fueled by conscription and ideological fervor. Fanatical loyalty makes them dangerous opponents.

Dominion Republic

Federal Alliance

A federal union of smaller states banded together for mutual protection and prosperity. Operates on shared sovereignty principles, with member states retaining autonomy while cooperating on defense, trade, and foreign policy.

11-Framework Analysis

1. Historical

Formed a century ago when seven small states united to resist absorption. Federal compact expanded twice.

2. Political

Federal system dividing power between central government and member states. Federal Assembly represents the people.

3. Mental

Education varies by state but emphasizes civic responsibility. Federal government supports universities and research.

4. Spiritual

Religious freedom guaranteed. Member states have diverse spiritual traditions. Interfaith dialogue encouraged.

5. Social

Diverse social structures between states. Federal citizenship coexists with state identities. Free movement allowed.

6. Cultural

Rich cultural diversity celebrated. Federal programs support cultural exchange between states.

7. Economical

Common market allows free trade. Federal currency supplements state currencies. Combined economic weight is significant.

8. Physiological

Health standards coordinated federally. Medical research shared. Public health emergencies addressed through coordination.

9. Territorial

Member states retain traditional territories. Federal districts house central institutions. Infrastructure projects promote integration.

10. Technological

Research collaboration accelerates innovation. Federal standards ensure compatibility. States specialize and share expertise.

11. Military

Federal Defense Force combines units from all states. Each state maintains militia. Unified command ensures coordinated response.
