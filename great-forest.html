The Branches of the Tree

The Great Forest is the middle realm of the Eastern Plane, the Branches of the Tree of Order. It is the domain of the Second Persona — the cosmic principle of Knowledge, Time, and Space — and the home of the Keepers, the wardens who guard thresholds, roads, gates, and the passages between worlds. Unlike the celestial heights of the Apex or the somber depths of the Mist, the Great Forest is a place of transition: a forest of paths, not merely of trees.

To walk the Great Forest is to walk between moments, between places, between possibilities. The Keepers maintain the integrity of these passages, ensuring that the boundaries between the realms remain distinct yet traversable. At its heart lies the Nexus of Ways, where all thresholds converge, guarded by Portalis, the Grand Keeper of the Western Gate.

The Keepers

The Keepers are the servants of the Second Persona, each responsible for a specific threshold, road, or passage. They are the guardians of continuity, ensuring that the flow of time and space does not unravel. Some Keepers are ancient beyond mortal reckoning; others are more recent appointments. The most active Keepers in the saga are those who guard the major passages of the Eastern Plane.

Goldroad

Region Keeper of Lux Imperio Border

An old woman in golden cloth who guards the threshold between Arcadia and the Northern realm. She guided Lucius Arogzan at his departure from Arcadia in Year 7003.

Orientalis

Keeper of Eastern Passages

Guardian of the thresholds leading into Chuugoku. Appeared to Lucius during his wandering and to the 25th Guardian in later eras. His form shifts like the eastern wind.

Occidens

Keeper of Western Gate

Warden of the passage into Avalon. She stands at the edge of the misty kingdoms, her form half-seen, her voice the sound of distant waves.

Meridionalis

Keeper of Southern Gate

Guardian of the threshold into Helron. He appears as a figure of precise geometry, his voice carrying the hum of calculations.

Portalis

Grand Keeper of the Western Gate · Keeper of the Nexus

Ancient being robed in silver and grey, face hidden behind a mask of polished wood. He guards the Nexus of Ways at the heart of the Great Forest. It was Portalis who guided Lucius to the Fourth Lord's Sanctuary.

Hierarchy of the Keepers

  • Grand Keepers — Oversee great trans-plane thresholds (e.g., Portalis).
  • Realm Keepers — Guard passageways between realms (Orientalis, Occidens, Meridionalis).
  • Regional Keepers — Wardens of great roads, passes, highways (Goldroad).
  • City Keepers — Guardians of gates and sacred urban thresholds.
  • Threshold Keepers — Wardens of doors, bridges, fords, and liminal moments.

"The Keepers do not bar the way; they ensure that the way remains."

The Eighth Clue: Center (ARTIFICE)

In Book 4 (The Wanderer's Path), after receiving the seventh clue in the Mist, Lucius Arogzan ascended to the Great Forest. Here, guided by Portalis, he reached the Nexus of Ways — the convergence point of all thresholds. The eighth clue was not an object to be found, but the gate itself. By placing the seven collected clues into the seven gaps of the gate, Lucius completed the circle and opened the way to the Sanctuary, the domain of the Fourth Lord.

The eighth clue represented ARTIFICE — the node of craft, creation, tools, and intention. The lesson of the Great Forest: "The center is not a place you find, but a relation you make." The Wanderer's Path did not end at a destination; it ended at a condition — becoming the center, the space between.

The Nexus of Ways

At the heart of the Great Forest lies the Nexus of Ways — a clearing where all thresholds meet. Here, the paths of the Eastern Plane converge: roads from Lux Imperio, Chuugoku, Avalon, and Helron; passages to the Celestial Realm, the Mist, and the Meridian Domain; and even faint traces of the Western Plane's influence. The Nexus is guarded by Portalis, who tests all who seek to pass.

The gate that leads to the Sanctuary requires seven clues to open — a safeguard placed by the 10th Guardian, Ada, to ensure that only one who has walked the full Wanderer's Path may enter the Fourth Lord's domain. When the seven clues are placed, the gate reveals the eighth: the center itself, the artifice of relation.
