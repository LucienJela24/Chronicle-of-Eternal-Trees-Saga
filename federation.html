Western Plane Federal Alliance

Federation of Sixteen Independent Nations

A grand alliance of sovereign states united by treaty, trade, and common defense against the Abyssal threat

16 Member Nations Federal Council Unified Defense

Overview

The Federation of Sixteen Independent Nations represents one of the most ambitious political experiments in the Western Plane—a voluntary alliance of sovereign states that have chosen to pool certain powers while retaining individual sovereignty. Founded in response to the growing Abyssal threat, the Federation has proven remarkably durable despite the diverse interests of its member states.

Each member nation maintains its own government, laws, and military forces, but coordinates with other members through the Federal Council on matters of trade, defense, and foreign policy. The Federation Charter establishes common standards for human rights, trade practices, and mutual defense obligations.

The Federation's success has made it a model for other regional organizations, though few have achieved the same level of integration. Its combined military forces represent a significant power in the Western Plane, and its economic cooperation has created prosperity that benefits all members.

Organizational Framework Analysis

1. Historical Framework

Founded after the Second Abyssal Incursion demonstrated the need for coordinated defense. The original Charter was signed by eight nations, with eight more joining over subsequent decades. The Federation has survived numerous challenges, including member disputes, economic crises, and military threats.

2. Political Framework

Federal system with power divided between member nations and federal institutions. The Federal Council serves as the primary legislative body, with each nation having representation proportional to its population. The High Commissioner acts as chief executive, elected by the Council for renewable terms.

3. Mental Framework

Education systems vary by member nation but share common standards established by the Federal Educational Council. Critical thinking, civic education, and multilingualism are emphasized. The Federal University system allows students to study across national boundaries.

4. Spiritual Framework

Religious freedom is guaranteed by the Charter, and member nations maintain diverse spiritual traditions. The Federation maintains no official religion, though various faiths have influence in different regions. Interfaith dialogue is encouraged as a means of promoting unity.

5. Social Framework

Social structures vary by member nation, but the Charter establishes common standards for rights and freedoms. Free movement between member nations allows citizens to live, work, and study across the Federation. A common citizenship supplements national identities.

6. Cultural Framework

Rich cultural diversity is celebrated as a Federation strength. Federal cultural programs promote exchange and understanding between nations. A common artistic tradition has emerged alongside national cultures, creating a distinctive "Federation style" in architecture, music, and visual arts.

7. Economical Framework

Common market allows free trade between member nations with standardized regulations. The Federal Bank manages a common currency used alongside national currencies. Economic development programs assist less prosperous regions. Combined, the Federation represents one of the largest economies in the Western Plane.

8. Physiological Framework

Federal health standards ensure minimum care levels across all member nations. The Federal Health Organization coordinates responses to epidemics and medical emergencies. Medical professionals can practice across national boundaries, facilitating the exchange of expertise.

9. Territorial Framework

The Federation spans diverse territories across the Western Plane, from coastal regions to interior plains and mountain ranges. Federal territories include shared infrastructure like highways, canals, and border fortifications. The capital district, neutral territory, houses federal institutions.

10. Technological Framework

Federal research programs coordinate technological development across member nations. The Federal Institute of Technology conducts advanced research and trains engineers and scientists. Standardized technical specifications facilitate interoperability of infrastructure and military equipment.

11. Military Framework

Combined military forces operate under unified command during joint operations. Member nations maintain national forces for internal security and contribute to federal forces for external defense. The Federal High Command coordinates strategy and operations, with officers from all member nations serving at all levels.
