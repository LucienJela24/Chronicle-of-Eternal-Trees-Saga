Overview

The Federation of Sixteen Independent Nations represents one of the most ambitious political experiments in the Western Plane—a voluntary alliance of sovereign states that have chosen to pool certain powers while retaining individual sovereignty. Founded in response to the growing Abyssal threat, the Federation has proven remarkably durable despite the diverse interests of its member states.

Each member nation maintains its own government, laws, and military forces, but coordinates with other members through the Federal Council on matters of trade, defense, and foreign policy. The Federation Charter establishes common standards for human rights, trade practices, and mutual defense obligations.

Castelmar — Shield of the Federation

Seat of the Serene Fleet · Guardian of the Wolfwash Strait

Castelmar serves as the Federation's primary naval fortress, commanding the critical Wolfwash Strait. The Serene Fleet's seven fleet basins — from the 1st Rescue Headquarters to the 7th Song — provide unparalleled maritime defense. The Blue Silence Academy trains officers, while the Admiralty House coordinates fleet operations across all sixteen nations.

Organizational Framework Analysis

1. Historical

Founded after the Second Abyssal Incursion demonstrated the need for coordinated defense. The original Charter was signed by eight nations, with eight more joining over subsequent decades. The Federation has survived numerous challenges over centuries of cooperation.

2. Political

Federal system with power divided between member nations and federal institutions. The Federal Council serves as the primary legislative body. The High Commissioner acts as chief executive, elected by the Council.

3. Mental

Education systems vary by member nation but share common standards. Critical thinking, civic education, and multilingualism are emphasized. The Federal University system allows cross-national study.

4. Spiritual

Religious freedom guaranteed by the Charter. Member nations maintain diverse spiritual traditions. No official religion exists at the federal level. Interfaith dialogue encouraged for unity.

5. Social

Social structures vary by nation, but the Charter establishes common rights and freedoms. Free movement allows citizens to live, work, and study across the Federation. Common citizenship supplements national identities.

6. Cultural

Rich cultural diversity celebrated as a Federation strength. Federal programs promote exchange between nations. A common artistic tradition has emerged alongside national cultures.

7. Economical

Common market with free trade between member nations. The Federal Bank manages a common currency. Economic development programs assist less prosperous regions. Combined, the Federation is one of the largest economies in the Western Plane.

8. Physiological

Federal health standards ensure minimum care levels. The Federal Health Organization coordinates epidemic responses. Medical professionals can practice across national boundaries.

9. Territorial

The Federation spans diverse territories across the Western Plane, from coastal regions to interior plains. Federal territories include shared infrastructure. The capital district houses federal institutions.

10. Technological

Federal research programs coordinate technological development. The Federal Institute of Technology conducts advanced research. Standardized specifications facilitate interoperability of infrastructure and equipment.

11. Military

Combined forces operate under unified command during joint operations. Member nations maintain national forces and contribute to federal forces. The Federal High Command coordinates strategy with officers from all sixteen nations.
