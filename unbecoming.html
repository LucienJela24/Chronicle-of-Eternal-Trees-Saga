The Unbecoming

An enigmatic faction whose nature, goals, and very existence remain shrouded in mystery

Unknown Origins Secretive Hidden Agenda

Overview

The Unbecoming is perhaps the most mysterious faction in the Eternal Trees Saga. Little is known about their origins, structure, or ultimate goals. They appear to operate in the shadows, influencing events from behind the scenes rather than openly asserting power.

What is known comes primarily from intelligence reports, whispered rumors, and the occasional encounter with their agents. The Unbecoming seems to recruit from across the planes, drawing individuals who have become disillusioned with existing power structures or who seek something beyond what conventional factions offer.

Some scholars speculate that the Unbecoming represents a philosophical movement as much as a political faction—that their name refers to a state of being rather than a conventional organization. Others believe they are a front for darker forces, manipulating events to serve inscrutable ends. Until more information comes to light, the true nature of the Unbecoming remains one of the great mysteries of the age.

What is Known

Agents and Operatives

The Unbecoming appears to operate through individual agents rather than conventional military forces. These operatives have been encountered in various locations across both planes, often in positions where they can influence important decisions. They are typically highly capable individuals with diverse skills ranging from diplomacy to combat to magic.

Network of Influence

Intelligence suggests the Unbecoming has cultivated connections across many existing factions. Their agents may hold positions in governments, military forces, merchant houses, and other organizations. This network allows them to gather information and potentially influence events without revealing their own involvement.

Philosophical Elements

Recovered documents suggest the Unbecoming is guided by a philosophy that questions conventional notions of identity, existence, and purpose. Their name may refer to a concept of transcending one's current state—"unbecoming" what one is to become something else. Whether this is spiritual, philosophical, or something more sinister remains unclear.

Secrecy and Security

The Unbecoming maintains extraordinary operational security. Captured agents have been known to die rather than reveal information, suggesting either fanatical loyalty or fear of consequences. Communications are encrypted using methods that have resisted decryption by the best codebreakers of both planes.

Theories and Speculation

The Transcendence Theory

Some scholars believe the Unbecoming seeks to help individuals transcend their mortal limitations, achieving a higher state of existence. This theory suggests they are essentially a spiritual or philosophical movement rather than a political faction.

The Manipulation Theory

Others argue that the Unbecoming is a front for powerful hidden interests—perhaps Abyssal forces, ancient beings, or entities from beyond the known planes. According to this theory, their philosophical trappings mask darker purposes.

The Reaction Theory

Some believe the Unbecoming emerged as a reaction to the failures of existing power structures—disillusioned individuals who have concluded that conventional approaches cannot address the fundamental challenges facing the world.

The Ancient Order Theory

A minority view holds that the Unbecoming is far older than it appears—perhaps dating back to the ancient civilization that preceded the current era. According to this theory, they have always operated in secret, guiding events from the shadows.
