The Abyss of Stillness

Tartarus is not a plane of fire and torment, but the deepest, stillest part of The Mist — a prison dimension of eternal silence where beings who cannot be killed are sealed. It is neither life nor death, but a state of suspended consequence: a place where the weight of memory is the only sentence, and the absence of time is the only warden.

Maintained by the Keepers and Death Reapers, Tartarus holds those whose corruption was so profound that even death could not contain them. Here, in absolute stillness, the reformed Dark Emperors serve their sentences in peaceful meditation, their Void-corruption burned out centuries ago. The Inquisitor Redford Heinsforth was also cast here after his trial.

The Dark Emperors (Reformed)

Tomoyama — Former Demon Emperor (Homura-ke, East)

Bronze skin faded to grey; white-hot eyes dimmed to embers. Meditates in silence. Chi barely flows — by choice. Speaks rarely: "The silence breaks those who cannot let go."

Kross — Former Shadow Emperor (Avalon, West)

Void-black eyes now grey; silver tears dried. Observes the stillness. Speaks of the Eye: "It sees what we could not."

Marcus — Former Spawn Emperor (Helron, South)

Pale skin; organic eyes regrown; mechanical voice softened. Reflects on Guardian intervention: "You intervened. That is what matters."

The Inquisitor

Grand Marshal Redford Heinsforth

Once the supreme authority of the Inquisitorial Office, Heinsforth abused his mandate to persecute the Tribune, conducted illegal experiments in the Depths of Abyss, and declared the Three Legendary Guardians "irrelevant." Arrested in Year 8018 (4th Myriad), he was tried for over two centuries and sentenced to eternal imprisonment in Tartarus.

Unlike the Emperors, Heinsforth's corruption was not Void-borne but systemic — a purity of purpose that consumed all relation. In Tartarus, he serves as a cautionary tale: the tyrant who believed efficiency excused atrocity.

Stillness, Not Torment

Tartarus is not a place of active punishment. There are no Jailers inflicting pain, no Pits of Fire or Ice. The sentence is stillness — the complete absence of change, sound, or interaction. Prisoners are sealed in individual chambers where time does not pass and no voice reaches them except their own memories.

This is the only way to contain beings whose essence cannot be destroyed. The Void-corruption that once empowered the Dark Emperors has long since burned out; they remain not because they are still dangerous, but because the consequences of their actions are eternal. Their peaceful meditation is both penance and witness.

"The silence breaks those who cannot let go." — Tomoyama

Lucius Arogzan's Visit (Year 8223, 4th Myriad)

After the sentencing of the Inquisitor, Lucius Arogzan (25th Guardian, the Wanderer) visited Tartarus briefly. He spoke with the reformed Dark Emperors, who confirmed that their corruption had burned out, leaving only the weight of memory. Kross spoke of the Eye: "It sees what we could not." Marcus reflected on the Guardian Domain's intervention: "You intervened. That is what matters."

Lucius left Tartarus with a deeper understanding of consequence: even those who have been forgiven by the cosmos must still bear the stillness of their choices. The Emperors' peaceful acceptance stood in stark contrast to Heinsforth's defiant silence.
