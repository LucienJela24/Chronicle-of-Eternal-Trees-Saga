The Old Tree

The Boundary-Wood · Last Remnant of the Wild of Aletheia · The Real Earth

Liminal Space Elder Wood Cosmic Archetype of Earth

The Last Witness of the Wild

The Old Tree is not a primordial source of all life, but the last remnant of the Wild of Aletheia — the Observer faction's sacred forest that was destroyed realm by realm during the Civil War of the Gods. Preserved in the Meridian Domain (the space between the Tree of Order and the Tree of Chaos), it stands as the boundary-wood, the eternal witness, and the cosmic archetype of Earth — specifically, the ancient elder groves and yew forests of Oxfordshire, England.

Unlike the Two Trees that seed the Eastern and Western Planes, the Old Tree does not create new realities. It remembers. Its roots hold the memory of the Earlier Gods' sacrifice, its bark carries the echoes of the Observers' genocide, and its leaves whisper the true history that the "Noble Withdrawal" myth was designed to bury. It is the liminal space that prevents Order and Chaos from consuming each other prematurely.

The Elder Wood of Oxfordshire

The Old Tree is the cosmic archetype of the real Earth — not a metaphor, but a harmonic resonance. The elder groves, ancient yews, and boundary-woods of Oxfordshire are its "microcosmic reflections." The surname Elder (from Old English ældre wudu, "elder wood") marks a lineage of boundary-tree tenders who, unwittingly, maintained the connection between Earth and the Meridian Domain for generations.

This is why Dr. Lucien George Elder was summoned to become the Fourth Librarian: his family's centuries of tending boundary-trees made him uniquely resonant with the Old Tree. His middle name, George (Greek geōrgos, "earth-worker," "tender of trees"), was a prophecy fulfilled when he became the permanent custodian of the Old Tree in the Meridian Domain.

The Keeper: Lucien Jela (Elder)

In Absolute Year 49,663 (5th Myriad), after nearly 30,000 years as Fourth Librarian, Lucien Jela retired to the Meridian Domain for permanent custodianship of the Old Tree. Accompanied by Eida Jada Copy XII, he tends the boundary-wood, ensuring that the Two Trees do not consume each other before the Convergence.

It was here, in the space between the Trees, that Lucien finally met his former student — Lucius Arogzan, the 25th Guardian, who had been reborn in the Eastern Plane after dying in the same Oxford traffic accident that preceded Lucien's summoning. Their reunion is the emotional core of the Convergence: the Librarian and the Wanderer, two souls from the Elder Wood, finally together.

Place in the Cosmic Order

The Boundary

The Old Tree stands between the Tree of Order (Eastern Plane) and the Tree of Chaos (Western Plane), maintaining the distinction that allows relationship without merger. Without it, the Two Trees would collapse into each other, ending both planes.

The Witness

The Old Tree remembers what the Two Trees have forgotten: the sacrifice of the Earlier Gods, the genocide of the Observers, and the true history of the Primordial Era that the "Covenant of Lies" was designed to conceal.

The Earth Connection

The Old Tree is the cosmic archetype of Earth. The elder groves of Oxfordshire are its reflections. When Lucien Elder returned to Earth in the 6th Myriad, he carried a crystallized seed from the Old Tree, bringing the boundary-wood home.

Two Souls from the Elder Wood

The Old Tree is the silent witness to the most profound reunion in the saga: the meeting of Lucien Jela (the Fourth Librarian) and Lucius Arogzan (the 25th Guardian) in the Meadow Realm during the Convergence. Two men from Oxford, both connected to the Elder Wood — one summoned as a Librarian, one reborn as a Guardian — finally meet after fifty thousand years of parallel paths.

Lucien gives Lucius a crystallized seed from the Old Tree, containing the memory of Earth and of the teacher he couldn't save. Lucius carries it back to the Eastern Plane, where it becomes the foundation of the Eye Practice and the Watchers' training — the cataloging, organizing impulse of a digital archivist, preserved across death and rebirth.
